Reading the diagrams

  • Valley fold — fold toward you, along this line.
  • Mountain fold — fold away from you, behind the sheet.
  • Existing crease — already folded, shown for reference.
  • Cut line — the rare design that wants scissors.
  • Movement arrow — which part moves, and where.

The golden rules

  • Crease like you mean it — run a fingernail or ruler edge along every fold.
  • Symmetry beats everything. Left and right halves must mirror exactly.
  • Fold on a hard, flat surface. Your lap is where good planes go to die.
  • Follow the order — fold sequences build on earlier creases.
  • Refold rather than repair. Paper forgives one retry.

Throwing & tuning

  • Darts: hard and flat, at shoulder height, like a javelin.
  • Distance: throw up at roughly 40–45° with maximum smooth power.
  • Gliders: a gentle, level push — let the wings do the work.
  • Dives? bend both trailing edges up 2–3 mm. Climbs & stalls? bend them down a touch.
  • Veers left/right? check symmetry first, then bend the rudder (rear fuselage) away from the turn.
